Anatomy of the Thyroid

1. Thyroid Gland
Located in front of the trachea; produces hormones that regulate metabolism.
2. Cricoid Cartilage
Forms a complete circle around the trachea; supports the airway.
3. Cricothyroid Ligament
Connects the cricoid and thyroid cartilages; plays a role in voice pitch.
4. Thyroid Cartilage
Largest laryngeal cartilage; protects the vocal cords and forms the "Adam's apple."
5. Thyrohyoid Membrane
Connects thyroid cartilage to the hyoid bone; supports the larynx.
6. Hyoid Bone
A unique bone that supports the tongue; important for swallowing and speech.
7. Epiglottis
A flap that prevents food from entering the trachea during swallowing.
8. Trachea
The windpipe that conducts air to the lungs; filters and moistens air.
9. Esophagus
Tube that connects the throat to the stomach; transports food.
10. Parathyroid Gland
Regulates calcium levels in the blood; located behind the thyroid.
